པར་རིས་ཐོགས་ནས་ལོ་རྒྱུས།

A Pictorial Journey

འདི་ནི་རག་ར་ཐུབ་བསྟན་ཆོས་དར་བཀྲས་མཐོང་རིན་པོ་ཆེ་མཆོག་གི་མཛད་རྣམ་མདོར་བསྡུས་ཤིག་ཡིན་ཞིང་། ཁོང་གི་བོད་ནང་གི་ཐོག་མའི་མཛད་པ་ནས་བཙན་བྱོལ་དུ་ཕེབས་པའི་བར་གྱི་མཛད་པ་རྣམས་མཚོན་ཡོད། པར་དང་རི་མོའི་ལམ་ནས་ཁོང་གི་མཁས་པ་དང་། དགེ་རྒན། སྒྱུ་རྩལ་བ། བོད་ཀྱི་རིག་གཞུང་དང་ནང་པའི་ཤེས་རབ་ལ་གུས་ཞབས་ཆེ་བའི་ཞབས་ཞུ་བ་བཅས་ཀྱི་མཛད་རྣམ་རྗེས་སུ་འདེད་ཐུབ། པར་རེ་རེས་དུས་ཚོད་ཀྱི་སྐར་ཆ་རེ་མཚོན་ཞིང་། ཁོང་གི་མི་ཚེས་བོད་ཀྱི་ཤེས་རིག་གི་ཕྱུག་བདག་མཚོན་པ་དང་འབྱུང་འགྱུར་མི་རབས་ཚོའི་ཆེད་དུ་དེ་དག་སྲུང་འཛིན་བྱེད་པའི་དམ་བཅའ་བརྟན་པོ་མཚོན་ཡོད།

This gallery presents a visual timeline of Rakra Thubten Choedar Tethong Rinpoche's remarkable life, spanning from his early years in Tibet to his transformative years in exile. Through these photographs and images, we trace his journey as a scholar, teacher, artist, and devoted servant to Tibetan culture and Buddhist wisdom. Each image captures a moment in time, telling the story of a man whose life embodied the rich heritage of Tibetan civilization and his commitment to preserving it for future generations.

1928: Rakra Rinpoche was born in 1925 (Fire Ox Year) to Gyurme Gyatso Tethong, then Governor of Derge (Derge chikyap) and Dolma Tsering nee Rong Dikyiling (d/o Dikyiling Sawang Tsering Rabten). The boy was named Rigzin Namgyal by Khenchen Ngawang Samten Lodroe (1868-1931) of the Great Monastery of Derge. At the age of two he was recognized as the 6th Rakra incarnate of Pakshoe monastery in Kham.

1940: Rakra Rinpoche during his monastic education years, dedicated to rigorous scholarly pursuits

1949: Rakra Rinpocheserving as Gyegoe (disciplinary master) during Monlam festival, a significant honor

1950: Rakra Rinpochewith his influential teacher Gendun Choephel, a towering figure in Tibetan scholarship

1950: Rinpocheat the Potala Palace during Tibet's final years before major political changes

1956-60: Rinpoche serving at All India Radio, Tibetan service in Delhi, broadcasting to reach Tibetan audiences

August 17, 1960: Rakra Rinpoche's first day at Pestalozzi Children's Village in Trogen, Switzerland, beginning his service to refugee children

October 2, 1960: Arrival of the first group of Tibetan refugee children at Pestalozzi Children's Village

April 8, 1961: Official inauguration day of Yambhu Lagang, the Buddhist chapel and spiritual sanctuary Rakra Rinpoche built

April 8, 1961: Celebration of the inauguration of Yambhu Lagang with community members and supporters

April 8, 1961: Community gathering at the inauguration of Yambhu Lagang, celebrating this spiritual milestone

1973: Rakra during his productive years of teaching and cultural preservation in Switzerland

1973: His Holiness the 14th Dalai Lama's visit, a significant occasion for Rakra and the Tibetan community

1986: Rakra teaching the final group of Tibetan refugee children, passing on his wisdom and traditions

1988: Rakra's final move to Rapperswil-Jona, a new chapter in his continued service to the Tibetan community
